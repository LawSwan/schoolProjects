Leonhard Euler (1707-1783)

Leonhard Euler was one of the greatest mathematicians of all time. He wrote over 800 different books and papers on a wide range of topics in mathematics. Euler's mentor, Jean Bernoulli, recognized his exceptional talent early on.

Euler made significant contributions to many areas of mathematics. His works include Introductio in analysin infinitorum (1748) and Lettres à une princesse dâ€™Allemagne (1768-1772).

Euler introduced many notations and concepts in mathematics, including the use of the symbols e and π.

The Most Beautiful Equation in Math?

Euler's Equation combines several fundamental mathematical constants:

eiπ + 1 = 0

This equation links five of the most important numbers in mathematics:

The number e (Euler's number),

The number π (Pi),

The number i (the imaginary unit),

The number 1, and

The number 0.

The Mathematical Intelligencer ranked it among the "most beautiful equations".
